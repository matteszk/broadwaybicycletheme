Class & Seminar Details

Basic Class

The Co-ed Basic Class is an introductory course intended for those with little or no bicycle mechanic experience. Each class lasts two hours and meets once per week for 6 weeks. The class is divided into lecture and hands-on learning where the student works on their own bicycle.

Topics include

  • Week 1 – Fixing a flat
  • Week 2 – Front hub adjustment
  • Week 3 – Wheel Truing
  • Week 4 – Brake Adjustment
  • Week 5 – Sizing and replacing a chain
  • Week 6 – Rear derailleur adjustment

Wheel building

Want to learn how to build your own wheel? The Wheelbuilding class takes you step by step through the process.

Topics include

  • Week 1 – Parts ordering and basic theory
  • Week 2 – NO CLASS to allow time for parts to arrive
  • Week 3 – Wheel lacing
  • Week 4 – Wheel tensioning/truing
  • Week 5 – Final tensioning and truing

Disc Brake Seminar

In this seminar we will go over both mechanical and hydraulic disc brake systems in detail

Topics include

  • General Maintenance and Upkeep
  • Pad Replacement
  • Rotor Replacement
  • Caliper Adjustment
  • Hydraulic Brake Bleed

A few things to know about our classes

Wear clothes that you don’t mind getting grease on.

Wear closed-toe shoes.

You must bring your own bike to work on.

You must be 18 years of age or older to take our classes.

If you need to cancel your enrollment you must do so at least 2 weeks before the class starts for a refund. After that, no refund will be issued.

Completing our class series earns you 10% off parts and accessories at Broadway Bicycle for life!

FTW Classes

FTW (femme/trans/women) classes are identical to the co-ed classes except that it is taught by women/transfolks/femme mechanics in an intentional environment meant to give space to people who want to learn bike mechanics and are not cismen.

Offsite Lessons

We also do off site work—Companies, events and organizations can hire us for events or on location trainings and/or tune-ups.
